AI + Cultural Heritage

"Year Painting Studio" - AI-Powered Cultural Heritage Preservation Project

Combining traditional culture with modern AI technology to innovatively protect and inherit Chinese New Year painting art

Project Background & Motivation

Traditional Chinese New Year paintings represent one of China's most widely recognized national intangible cultural heritage projects, but they face inheritance challenges in modern society. We chose to use AI technology to empower New Year painting creation, allowing intangible cultural heritage to connect with modern life and continue the vitality and joy of traditional culture through innovative expressions.

Technical Innovation

We proposed an Adaptive Deep Attention Style Transfer model (ADAST), combining Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) with VGG-19 pre-trained models. The model combines low-level texture information with high-level semantic information at each layer through adaptive attention normalization (AdaAttN) modules to achieve precise style transfer.

Dataset & Training

Using the MSCOCO dataset (approximately 330,000 images) for content images and the WikiArt dataset (approximately 81,000 artworks) for style images. All images are uniformly loaded as 512×512 and randomly cropped to 256×256 for data augmentation, ensuring the model can handle diverse input content.

Practical Application Results

The project successfully created a user-friendly interface allowing users to upload daily photos and select New Year painting styles, generating personalized New Year painting works in seconds. The system not only maintains the artistic characteristics of traditional New Year paintings but also incorporates modern elements, achieving perfect integration of cultural inheritance and innovation.

Machine Learning Research

Robustness and Vulnerability of Perceptrons and Support Vector Machines

Deep exploration of theoretical foundations and practical performance of linear classification algorithms, achieving Grade A in Pioneer Academics research project

Research Problem & Significance

Linear classification models are fundamental building blocks of machine learning, laying the foundation for deep neural networks. This study deeply analyzes the robustness and vulnerability of perceptron algorithms and support vector machines under various data conditions, providing theoretical guidance for model selection in practical applications.

Theoretical Contributions

We provided detailed proofs of Novikoff's convergence theorem, demonstrating the convergence guarantee of perceptrons on linearly separable data, while analyzing the cycling theorem to explain the failure mechanism of perceptrons on non-linearly separable data. For SVMs, we analyzed their dual formulation and kernel trick, demonstrating their superiority under the maximum margin principle.

Experimental Design

Designed a comprehensive experimental suite including sparsity tests, noise ratio tests, data range tests, biased data tests, and margin tests. Used 10-fold cross-validation on synthetic datasets with 500 samples across 5 different difficulty levels, ensuring statistical significance of results.

Key Findings

Research found that while SVMs consistently achieve the highest accuracy under various conditions, certain perceptron variants (particularly voted perceptrons combined with anti-noise mechanisms) remain competitive in high-noise environments, providing effective alternatives for resource-constrained application scenarios.

Challenge Background

The Kaggle 3D Object Detection Challenge focused on biological imaging applications, specifically locating flagellar motors in 3D reconstructions of bacteria. This presented unique challenges in computer vision due to the complex 3D nature of biological structures and the need for precise localization in noisy microscopy data.

Technical Approach

Applied deep learning models and computer vision techniques to process 3D biological imaging data. The main challenge was precisely locating biological structures from noisy microscopy data, requiring development of specialized data preprocessing pipelines and neural network architectures suitable for 3D object detection.

Innovation Points

Developed novel data augmentation techniques specifically designed for 3D biological data, implemented custom loss functions that account for the volumetric nature of the data, and created ensemble methods combining multiple detection approaches to improve robustness against imaging artifacts.

Competition Performance

Achieved 22nd place among 390+ participants, demonstrating technical strength in the intersection of computer vision and bioinformatics. This achievement reflects deep understanding of complex 3D image processing and object detection tasks in challenging biological contexts.

Summer Program Experience Overview

Carnegie Mellon University is one of America's top music conservatories, and its summer program brings together outstanding young musicians from around the world. In this high-level academic environment, I had the opportunity to encounter cutting-edge contemporary music concepts and performance techniques, expanding my musical horizons.

Contemporary Work "Rain" Challenges

The performed contemporary clarinet solo "Rain" was an experimental work created by a fellow participant, employing sparse rubato feel and extended techniques such as flutter-tonguing. This piece challenged traditional performance methods, requiring performers to create unique melancholic atmospheres through precise tonal control and phrasing.

Mastering Extended Techniques

Flutter-tonguing is a commonly used extended technique in modern clarinet works that produces unique tonal effects. Through specialized practice and guidance, I mastered the control method for this technique, learning how to organically combine technique with musical expression, not merely for show.

Expansion of Artistic Concepts

This experience gave me profound understanding of contemporary music aesthetics, learning to transcend traditional interpretive frameworks and focus on shaping raw emotional texture through precise control of timbre and phrasing. This avant-garde musical approach has now been integrated into my future performance and composition work.
